Estructura de un Argumentative Essay

  1. Introducción: presentación del tema, planteamiento del problema y presentación de la tesis o idea principal del ensayo.
  2. Desarrollo: presentación de argumentos a favor y en contra del tema, utilizando evidencias y ejemplos para apoyarlos.
  3. Contraargumentos: refutación de los argumentos en contra de la tesis presentada.
  4. Conclusión: resumen de los principales puntos del ensayo y afirmación de la postura del autor.

Consejos para mejorar la escritura de un Argumentative Essay


  • Utilizar un lenguaje claro y preciso, evitando el uso de jerga o tecnicismos.
  • Asegurarse de tener una postura clara y sólida sobre el tema.
  • Utilizar evidencias y ejemplos fiables y actuales para apoyar los argumentos.
  • Considerar y refutar los posibles contraargumentos.
  • Revisar y corregir errores gramaticales y ortográficos antes de entregar el ensayo.

Argumentative Essay Writing: Steps to Follow


  1. Step 1: Choose a strong and debatable topic

    . The topic of your essay should be something that is debatable and has multiple perspectives, this way it will be easier for you to argue your point of view.

  2. Step 2: Research and gather evidence

    . Find credible sources that support your argument and also those that argue against it. Use this information to build a strong case for your point of view.

  3. Step 3: Create an outline

    . Organize your thoughts and ideas in a logical order, this will make it easier for you to write the essay and for the reader to follow your argument.

  4. Step 4: Write the introduction

    . Start by introducing the topic and providing background information. Then, state your thesis or main argument and preview the main points that you will cover in the essay.

  5. Step 5: Write the body paragraphs

    . Use one paragraph for each main point that supports your thesis. Use evidence and examples to back up your claims and consider any counterarguments and refute them.

  6. Step 6: Write the conclusion

    . Summarize the main points of the essay, restate your thesis, and leave the reader with a final thought to consider.

  7. Step 7: Proofread and edit

    . Review your essay for grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors. Make sure that your argument is clear and well-supported and that your writing is smooth and easy to read.

Tips for writing a strong argumentative essay

  • Stay focused on the topic and stick to your main argument.
  • Use evidence and examples to support your claims and counterarguments.
  • Use strong and persuasive language, avoid using fallacies and emotional language.
  • Be open-minded and consider other perspectives, but be prepared to refute them.
  • Revise and proofread your essay multiple times to ensure it is error-free and polished.

Example


The Debate on Gun Control: A Look at Both Sides


Introduction

The issue of gun control has been a controversial topic in the United States for decades. On one hand, there are those who argue that stricter gun control laws are necessary to reduce gun violence and keep citizens safe. On the other hand, there are those who argue that stricter gun control laws would infringe on the Second Amendment rights of American citizens and would not effectively address the root causes of gun violence.

Argument for stricter gun control laws

Proponents of stricter gun control laws argue that these laws would reduce the number of mass shootings and gun-related homicides. They point to countries with stricter gun laws, such as Japan and Australia, which have significantly lower rates of gun violence. Additionally, they argue that background checks and waiting periods would keep guns out of the hands of criminals and those with mental health issues.

Argument against stricter gun control laws

Opponents of stricter gun control laws argue that these laws would not effectively address the root causes of gun violence. They argue that criminals will still find ways to obtain guns, regardless of laws, and that law-abiding citizens should have the right to defend themselves. They also argue that the Second Amendment guarantees the right to bear arms and that stricter gun control laws would infringe on this right.

Counterargument

While it is true that criminals will still find ways to obtain guns, stricter gun control laws would make it more difficult for them to do so. Additionally, background checks and waiting periods would help keep guns out of the hands of those with a history of violence or mental health issues. Furthermore, the Second Amendment guarantees the right to bear arms, but it does not guarantee an unrestricted right to any and all firearms.

Conclusion

The issue of gun control is a complex and controversial one, with valid arguments on both sides. However, it is clear that stricter gun control laws would help reduce the number of gun-related deaths and keep citizens safe without infringing on the Second Amendment rights of law-abiding citizens. By implementing measures such as background checks and waiting periods, we can ensure that guns are not in the hands of those who should not have them, while still allowing responsible citizens the right to bear arms. Additionally, addressing the root causes of gun violence, such as mental health issues and poverty, must also be a priority in any comprehensive solution to this issue.
