Estructura de la escritura de cartas informales

La estructura básica de una carta informal incluye el encabezado, el cuerpo y la despedida. El encabezado incluye la fecha, el destinatario y la dirección. El cuerpo es el contenido principal de la carta, donde se incluyen los detalles y la información relevante. La despedida es la forma en que terminas la carta, puede incluir un saludo o un deseo.

Consejos y trucos para mejorar la escritura de cartas informales

  • Usa un lenguaje informal y amistoso.
  • Sigue una estructura lógica y ordenada.
  • Usa un tono adecuado para el destinatario y el propósito de la carta.
  • Revisa y corrige los errores gramaticales y ortográficos antes de enviar la carta.
  • Incluye solo la información relevante y necesaria.
  • Usa una despedida cálida y personalizada.
  • Usa una letra legible y un buen papel.

Informal Letter Writing: Steps to Follow

Informal letter writing is a way to communicate with friends, family, and other people you know in a casual and friendly manner. To write an effective informal letter, there are several steps you should follow:

Step 1: Determine the Purpose of the Letter

Before you begin writing, it's important to know the purpose of the letter. Is it to share news, ask for advice, or simply catch up with someone? Knowing the purpose will help you to structure the letter and choose the appropriate tone.

Step 2: Gather the Necessary Information

Make sure you have all the information you need before you begin writing. This will save you time and ensure that the letter is complete and accurate.

Step 3: Address the Recipient

Start your letter by addressing the recipient with a friendly and informal greeting. Use their name and include a personal touch, such as a shared memory or inside joke.

Step 4: Use a Friendly and Casual Tone

It's important to use a friendly and casual tone when writing informal letters. This means avoiding formal language and using colloquial expressions and contractions.

Step 5: Be Personal and Share News and Thoughts

Informal letters are a great way to stay in touch with friends and family, so be personal and share news and thoughts. Talk about your own experiences, ask about theirs and give them updates on your life. This helps to keep the letter interesting and engaging.

Step 6: End the Letter with a Positive Note

End the letter with a positive note, such as a closing salutation, a heartfelt expression of gratitude, or a kind remark. It's important to leave the recipient with a good feeling and make them look forward to your next correspondence.

Step 7: Proofread and Edit

Before sending the letter, proofread and edit it for any grammatical or spelling errors. This will ensure that the letter is clear and easy to understand.

Step 8: Sign and Seal

Finally, sign the letter with your name and add a seal if you like. This will give the letter a personal touch and make it more memorable.

By following these steps, you'll be able to write an effective and enjoyable informal letter that will be appreciated by the recipient.


Example


Letter to a Friend

Dear [Name],

I hope this letter finds you well. I just wanted to drop you a quick note to say how much I miss you and how much I hope you are doing great. I've been thinking about you a lot lately and I wanted to catch up with you.

What's new with you? How has life been treating you? I've been busy with work and some other things, but I am doing good. I recently went on a trip to [destination] and it was amazing! I wish you could have been there with me.

I also wanted to tell you about a new hobby I've picked up: [hobby]. It's been so much fun and I've met a lot of cool people. I think you would enjoy it too!

Anyway, I just wanted to reach out and check in with you. Let me know how you're doing and if you have time to catch up soon. I would love to hear from you.

Take care,

[Your Name]
